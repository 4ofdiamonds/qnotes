3) Suppose that $f'(x)=\frac{\sqrt{ x }-4}{x^4}$ for x>0, and f(1)=0. Find $f(4)$
$$\begin{align}
f'(x) & =\frac{\sqrt{ x }}{x^4}-\frac{4}{x^{-4}} \\
& =x^{-7/2}-4x^{-4} \\
f(x) & =-\frac{2}{5}x^{-5/2}+\frac{4}{3}x^{-3}+c \\
0 & =-\frac{2}{5}+\frac{4}{3}+c  \\
\implies  c & =\frac{2}{5}-\frac{4}{3} \\
 c & =-\frac{14}{15} \\
\therefore f(x) & =-\frac{2}{5}x^{-5/2}+\frac{4}{3}x^{-3}-\frac{14}{15} \\
f(4) & =-\frac{2}{5\sqrt{ 4^5 }}+\frac{3}{3 \cdot 4^3}-\frac{14}{15}
\end{align}$$
b) Suppose that $f'(x)=\frac{6}{1+x^2}$ and $f(0)=4$ Find $f(\sqrt{ 3 })$

$$\begin{align}
f'(x) & =\frac{6}{1+x^2} \implies f(x)=6\tan^{-1}x+c \\
f(0) & =4=6\tan^{-1}0+c \implies 4=c \\
f(\sqrt{ 3 }) & =6\arctan \sqrt{ 3 }+4 \\

\end{align}$$
