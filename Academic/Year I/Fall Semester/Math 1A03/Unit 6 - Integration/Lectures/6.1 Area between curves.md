# Example
Find the area of the region enclosed by y=sinx, y=cosx, x=0 and x=$\frac{\pi}{2}$
![[6.1 Area between curves-1762449142088.jpeg]]
$$\begin{align}
 & \int_{0}^{\pi/4}\cos x -\sin x\,dx + \int_{\frac{\pi}{4}}^0\sin x-\cos x\,dx \\
  & u=\frac{\pi}{4} \\
 & =2\int_{0}^{\pi/4} (\cos x-\sin x)\,dx \\

 & =2 (\sin x+\cos x)\Bigg|_{0}^{\pi/4} \\
\end{align}$$
# Integrate with respect to y
![[6.1 Area between curves-1762534065567.jpeg]]
$$\begin{align}
\begin{cases}
y^2=2x+6 \\
y=x-1
\end{cases}  & \implies (x-1)^2=2x+6 \\
 & \iff x^2-2x+1=2x+6 \\
 & \iff x^2-4x-5=0 \\
 & \iff (x-5)(x+1)=0 \\
 & \iff x=-1,5 \\
 & \iff y=-2,4
\end{align}$$

\end{align}$$
