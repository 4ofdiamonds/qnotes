# Determining Volumes
![[6.2 Volumes-1762534220297.jpeg]]
## Approximation of volume:
$$\begin{align}
v \simeq \sum_{i}^n A(x_{i})\Delta x
\end{align}$$
![[6.2 Volumes-1762534334768.jpeg]]
## General formula for volume:
$$\begin{align}
v=\int_{a}^bA(x)\,dx
\end{align}$$
Where A(x) is the area of the cross-section above x
## Application: volume of a sphere
![[6.2 Volumes-1762534411661.jpeg]]
$$\begin{align}
A(x) & =\pi(\sqrt{ r^2-x^2 })^2 \\
 & =\pi(r^2-x^2) \\
v & =\int_{-r}^{-r}A(x)\,dx \\
 & =\pi \int_{-r}^r(r^2-x^2)\,dx \\
 & \text{Since it is an even function} \\
 & =2\pi\left( r^2 x-\frac{x^3}{3} \right)\Bigg|_{0}^r \\
 & =\frac{4\pi r^3}{3}
\end{align}$$

# Solids of Revolution (2)
![[6.2 Volumes-1762535446487.jpeg]]
$$\begin{align}
A(x) & =(\pi x)^2-(\pi x^2)^2 \\
v & =\int_{0}^1A(x)\,dx \\
 & =\pi \int_{0}^1(x^2-x^4)\,dx \\
 & =\pi \left( \frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{5} \right) \\
 & =\frac{2\pi}{15}
\end{align}$$
# Example
- Find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating the region R around the line y=2
![[6.2 Volumes-1762879186163.jpeg]]
$$\begin{align}
A(x) & =\pi(2-x^2)^2-\pi(2-x)^2 \\
V & =\pi \int_{0}^1(2-x^2)^2-(2-x)^2\,dx \\
 & =\pi \int_{0}^1(x^4-4x^2-x^2-4x)\,dx \\
 & =\pi  \int_{0}^1 (x^4-5x^2+4x)\,dx \\
 & =\pi\left( \frac{1}{5}-5 \frac{1}{3}+4 \frac{1}{2} \right)
\end{align}$$
## Example
- Find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating the region R around the line x=-1
$$\begin{align}
A(y) & =\pi(1+\sqrt{ y })^2-\pi(1+y)^2 \\
v & = \int_{0}^1 A(y)\,dy \\
 & =\pi \int_{0}^1 (y+2\sqrt{ y }-y^2-2y)\,dy \\
 & =\pi \int_{0}^1 (-y^2-y+2y^{1/2})\,dy \\
 & = \pi\left( -\frac{1}{3}y^3-\frac{y^2}{2}+2 \frac{y^{3/2}}{\frac{3}{2}} \right)\Bigg|_{0}^1 \\
 & =\pi\left( -\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{2}+\frac{4}{3} \right) \\
 & =\frac{\pi}{2}
\end{align}$$
