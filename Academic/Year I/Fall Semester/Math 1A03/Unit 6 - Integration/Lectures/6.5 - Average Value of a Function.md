# Deriving the formula
![[6.5 - Average Value of a Function-1763051796040.jpeg]]
- We can approximate the average value $f_{\text{avg}}$ of f on the interval $[a,b]$ into n intervals $[x_{i-1},x_{i}]$, taking $x_{i} \in [x_{i-1}x_{i}$ and computing
$$\begin{align}
f_{\text{avg}} &  \approx \frac{f(x_{i}^*)+\dots+f(x_{m}^*)}{n} \\
 & = \frac{1}{ \underbrace{ n }_{ \Delta x }} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_{i}^*) \\
 & =\frac{1}{b-a}\sum_{i=1}^nf(x_{i}^*)\Delta x \\
\implies f_{\text{avg}} & =\frac{1}{b-a}\lim_{ n \to +\infty } \sum_{i=1}^nf(x_{i}^*)\Delta x \\
f_{\text{avg}} & =\frac{1}{b-a}\int _{a}^b f(x)\,dx
\end{align}$$
# MVT for intergrals
## Theorem
If f is continuous on $[a,b]$, then there exists a number c in $[a,b]$ such that
$$\begin{align}
f(c)=f_{\text{avg}}=\frac{1}{b-a}\int _{a}^b f(x)\,dx \iff (b-a)f(c)=\int _{a}^b f(x)\,dx
\end{align}$$
## Proof
$$\begin{align}
 & \text{Let }f(x)\text{ be a primitive function } f(x) \\
 & F'(x) =f(x) \\
 & \text{MVT for derivatives to F(x) and find} \\
 & c \in (a,b) \text{ such that } \\
 & f(c)=F'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}=\frac{1}{b-a}\int _{a}^b f(x)\,dx
\end{align}$$
