# $\int \sin^m x \cos^n x \,dx$
## Examples
$$\begin{align}
\int \cos^3x\,dx & = \int \cos x \cos^x \,dx \\
 & = \int \cos x(1-\sin^2x)\,dx \\ \\
u & =\sin x \\
du & =\cos x\,dx \\ \\
 & =\int (1-u^2)\,du \\
 & =u-\frac{u^3}{3}+c \\
 & =\sin x- \frac{\sin^3x}{3}+c
\end{align}$$
$$\begin{align}
\int \sin^5x\cos^2x\,dx & = \int \sin x (\sin^2)^2 \cos^2x \,dx \\
 & =\int \sin x(1-\cos^2x)^2 \cos^2x\,dx \\ \\
u & =\cos x \\
du & =-\sin x\,dx \\
 & =-\int(1-u^2)^2 u^2\,dx \\
 & =-\int (1-2u^2+u^4)u^2 \, du \\
 & = -\frac{u^3}{3}+2\frac{u^5}{5}-\frac{u^7}{7}+c \\
 & =-\frac{\cos^3x}{3}+2 \frac{\cos^5x}{5}-\frac{\cos^7x}{7}+c
\end{align}$$
$$\begin{align}
\int\sin^4x \,dx & =\int \frac{1}{2}(1-\cos 2x)^2\,dx \\
 & =\frac{1}{4}\int 1-2\cos 2x+ (\cos \underbrace{ 2x }_{ y })^2\,dx \\
 & =\frac{1}{4} \left( x-2 \frac{\sin 2x}{2} \right)+\int\frac{1}{2}(1+\cos(4x))\,dx \\
 & =\frac{1}{4}x-\frac{1}{4}\sin 2x+ \frac{1}{8} \left( x+ \frac{\sin 4x}{4} \right)+c
\end{align}$$
## Conclusion for $\int \sin^m x \cos^n x \,dx$
- m is odd -> u=cosx
- n is odd -> u=sinx
- m and n are even -> use half angle formulas
	- $\sin^2x=\frac{1}{2}(1-\cos (2x))$
	- $\cos^2x=\frac{1}{2}(1+\cos(2x))$
# $\int \sin(mx)\cos (nx)\,dx$
- Strategy: for $\int \sin(mx)\cos(mx)\,dx$, use the formulas
$$\begin{align}
\sin a\cos b=\frac{1}{2}(\sin (a-b)+\sin (a+b)) \\
\sin a\sin b=\frac{1}{2}(\cos (a-b)-\cos (a+b)) \\
\cos a\cos b=\frac{1}{2}(\cos (a-b)+\cos (a+b))
\end{align}$$
## Example
$$\begin{align}
\int \sin 4x\cos 5x\,dx & =\int \frac{1}{2}\sin (4x-5x)+\sin (4x+5x)\,dx \\
 & =\frac{1}{2}\int \sin (-x)\,dx+\int \sin (9x)\,dx \\
 & = \cos (-x)-\frac{\cos(9x)}{9}+c \\
 & =\frac{1}{2}\cos (x)-\frac{\cos (9x)}{9}+c
\end{align}$$
# $\int \tan^mx\sec^nx\,dx$
$$\begin{align}
\frac{1}{\cos ^2x}\cdot\sin ^2x+\cos ^2x & =1\cdot\frac{1}{\cos ^2x} \\
\tan ^2x+1 & =\sec ^2x
\end{align}$$
## Example
$$\begin{align}
\int \tan ^6x\sec ^4x \,dx & =\int \tan ^6x(1+\tan ^2x)\sec ^2x\,dx \\
 & =\int u^6(1+u^2)\,du \\
 & =\frac{u^7}{7}+\frac{u^9}{9}+c \\
 & =\frac{\tan ^7x}{7}+\frac{\tan ^9x}{9}+c
\end{align}$$
$$\begin{align}
\int \tan ^5x \sec ^7x\,dx & =\int(\tan ^2x)^2\sec ^6x\tan x\sec x\,dx \\
 & =\int(\sec ^2x-1)^2\sec ^6x\tan x\sec x\,dx \\
 & =\int(u^2-1)^2u^6\,du \\
 & =\frac{\sec ^{11}x}{11}-2\frac{\sec ^9x}{9}+\frac{\sec ^7x}{7}+c \\
\end{align}$$
$$\begin{align}
\tan ^3x\,dx  & =\int \tan ^2x\tan x\,dx \\
 & =\int(\sec ^2x-1)\tan x\,dx \\
 & =\int \tan x\sec ^2x\,dx-\int \tan x \,dx \\
 & = \int u\,du-\ln (\sec x) \\
 & =\frac{\tan ^2x}{2}-\ln(\sec x)+c
\end{align}$$
## Conclusion for $\int \tan^mx\sec^nx\,dx$
- m is odd -> u=secx
- n is even -> u=tanx
- m is even and n is odd -> adventure
$$\begin{align}
\int \sec x\,dx & =\int \sec \left( \frac{\sec x+\tan x}{\sec x+\tan x} \right)\,dx \\
 & =\int \frac{\sec ^2x+\sec x\tan x}{\sec x+\tan x} \\
 \\
u & =\sec x+\tan x \\
du & =\tan x\sec x+\sec ^2x\,dx \\
 \\
 & =\int \frac{1}{u}\,du \\
 & =\ln u+c \\
 & =\ln(\sec x+\tan x)+c
\end{align}$$
