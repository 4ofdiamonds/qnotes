## Motivation
### Example $\int\frac{3x^3+x}{x-1}\,dx$
![[7.4 Partial fractions-1764090444721.jpeg]]
$$\begin{align}
\int \frac{x^3+x}{x-1}\,dx & =\int x^2+x+2+\frac{2}{x-1}\,dx \\
 & =\frac{x^3}{3}+\frac{x^2}{2}+2x+2\ln\abs{x-1}+c
\end{align}$$
# Distinct Linear Factors
![[7.4 Partial fractions-1764090678064.jpeg]]
$$\begin{align}
2x^3+3x^2-2x & =x(2x^2+3x-2) \\
 & =x(2x-1)(x+2) \\
 \\
\frac{x^2+2x-1}{x(2x-1)(x+2)} & =\frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{2x-1}+\frac{C}{x+2} \\
 & =\frac{A(2x-1)(x+2)+Bx(x+2)+Cx(2x-1)}{x(2x-1)(x+2)} \\ \\

x^2+2x-1 &  =A(2x^2+3x-2)+B(x^2+2x)+C(2x^2-x) \\ \\

& \begin{array}{r}
x^2 \\
x \\
x^0
\end{array}\begin{cases}
1=2A+B+2C \\
2=3A+2B-C \\
-1=-2A
\end{cases} \\
\iff  & \begin{cases}
A=\frac{1}{2} \\
B=\frac{1}{5} \\
C=-\frac{1}{10}
\end{cases}
\end{align}$$
